RIZAL

Region: IV-A Calabarzon

Rizal is one of the Philippines first class provinces that belongs to CALABARZON (CA-vite, LA-guna, BA-tangas, R-izal, Que-ZON) Region or region IV-A in the island of Luzon. The province enjoys a natural beauty of nature and a perfect setting for investments, business establishments and settlements. It is bounded by Sierra Madre Mountain Ranges and Quezon Province in the east, the province Laguna in the soutwest, the province of Bulacan in the north, Metropolitan Manila in the west-northwest portion and by Laguna de Bay in south/south-western portion. Rizal is one of the neighboring provinces of Metro Manila. It takes an hour drive from major commercial districts and centers of Metropolis to reach the province's best places. Rizal is a green province which is the best place to unwind from crowded and polluted metropolis.

CAPTURE THE BEAUTY OF THE PROVINCE OF RIZAL

Beyond its landscapes, Rizal also holds deep historical and cultural significance. The province was named after the Philippine national hero José Rizal, whose legacy continues to inspire the Filipino people. Artistic and cultural landmarks like Pinto Art Museum and the famous Antipolo Cathedral reflect the province’s strong connection to art, faith, and tradition. With its combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and peaceful atmosphere, Rizal Province stands as one of the most beautiful and meaningful destinations in the Philippines.

HISTORY

How Rizal Province Came to Be

The history of Rizal Province is closely connected to the development of the areas surrounding Manila during the Spanish colonial period. Before becoming a province, many of its towns such as Antipolo, Morong, and Tanay were originally part of the old Province of Manila and Province of Laguna. These areas were mainly agricultural communities and served as important settlements for local Filipinos during Spanish rule.

Rizal Province was officially created on June 11, 1901, during the American colonial period through Act No. 137 of the Philippine Commission. The province was formed by combining several towns from Manila and Laguna and was named in honor of the Philippine national hero José Rizal, who was born in Calamba. The Americans established the town of Pasig as the first capital of the province..
